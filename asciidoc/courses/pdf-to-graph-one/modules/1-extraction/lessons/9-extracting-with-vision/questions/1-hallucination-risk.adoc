= Hallucination risk

A vision model corrects `EKRON-S206 741254888` to `ENRON-524674254886`. Why is this correction dangerous?

* [ ] The corrected text is longer than the original
* [ ] Vision models can't read numbers accurately
* [x] The model produced a plausible-looking identifier, but there's no way to verify if `524674254886` is the real number -- it likely isn't
* [ ] The original OCR text was correct and shouldn't have been changed

[TIP,role=hint]
.Hint
====
OCR makes character-level errors but doesn't invent content. What does a vision model do differently?
====

[TIP,role=solution]
.Solution
====
OCR errors are predictable character substitutions -- the wrong character, but based on what's on the page. Vision models infer meaning, which means they can fill in values they can't read with plausible but wrong content. The identifier `524674254886` looks right but was hallucinated. Use vision models for body text where small errors are tolerable, not for identifiers or dates where accuracy matters.
====
